# Adaptive question bank

Use these as branch prompts, not a script. Ask only the next decision that can change the initial architecture.

## Consent and scope

- Which existing artifacts may I inspect before asking you questions?
- Which information must stay local or must never be written to durable notes?
- May the setup create files only, or also edit existing files after approval?
- What is the maximum amount of workflow change you can realistically adopt in the next seven days?

## Outcomes

- Six weeks from now, what observable change would make this system unquestionably worthwhile?
- Which repeated frustration consumes the most judgment, time, or emotional energy?
- Who receives the value of your work, and what do they care about most?
- What kind of failure would make a faster workflow unacceptable?

## Work-stream discovery

- What recurring result do people rely on you to produce?
- What event or request starts that work?
- What information must be correct before you begin?
- Which decision in this stream requires the most expertise?
- What does the final deliverable look like and who approves it?
- How do you know it is truly done rather than merely sent?
- What from this work will matter again in three months?

## Quality and judgment

- Show one example you consider excellent. What specifically makes it excellent?
- Show one technically acceptable example you dislike. Why?
- What correction do you repeat to collaborators or agents?
- Where should the system generate alternatives instead of choosing immediately?
- Which quality dimension is easiest to forget under deadline pressure?

## Tools and evidence

- Which tool is authoritative for each kind of truth?
- Which sources change frequently enough to require freshness checks?
- Which operations are repeated exactly enough to become scripts?
- Where do handoffs currently lose context?
- Which tool or process are you unwilling to change?

## Human constraints

- When and where does this work happen: desk, mobile, meetings, workshop, site, studio, travel?
- What interruption pattern should the workflow survive?
- What accessibility, language, visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive needs should shape the interface?
- What level of explanation is useful before it becomes friction?
- What should the system do when energy or time is low?

## Knowledge

- Which lesson should be searchable next year?
- What evidence must accompany a claim before it enters durable knowledge?
- Which notes expire or require review?
- How do you naturally look for information: project, question, person, date, deliverable, or topic?
- Which current folders or naming patterns already work and must be preserved?

## Automation and risk

- Which actions are safe to execute locally without asking?
- Which actions require review because they communicate, publish, spend, delete, commit, or affect another person?
- Which actions should never be delegated?
- What is the rollback path when an automated change is wrong?
- Which compliance, safety, privacy, or professional standards apply?

## Adoption and governance

- Which one workflow should prove value first?
- How often should the system review its own performance?
- What evidence should be required before adding another skill?
- Who may change the workflow constitution?
- How long should an unused skill remain active before deprecation?
