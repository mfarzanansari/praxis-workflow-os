# Code of Conduct

Praxis is built for people with different professions, abilities, cultures, risk tolerances, and levels of technical experience. Participation must make that diversity safer and more useful.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas, behavior, evidence, and code—not a person's identity or competence.
- Explain unfamiliar terminology when context suggests it will help.
- Respect privacy, consent, attribution, licenses, and security boundaries.
- Disclose relevant conflicts, generated content, copied material, and third-party dependencies.
- Accept correction and update claims when evidence changes.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized conduct, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Deceptive demonstrations, fabricated benchmarks, hidden telemetry, malicious code, credential collection, or unsafe instructions.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions, spamming, or pressuring maintainers for unpaid support.
- Using project spaces to facilitate unauthorized access, surveillance, abuse, or evasion of safeguards.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content; request changes; temporarily restrict participation; or permanently ban contributors when necessary to protect the project and its community. Enforcement should be proportionate, documented privately where possible, and focused on safety rather than public punishment.

Report conduct concerns privately through the maintainer's GitHub profile. Do not disclose sensitive personal information in a public issue.
